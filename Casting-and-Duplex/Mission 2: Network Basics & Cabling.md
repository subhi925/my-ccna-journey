🛠️ Mission 2: Network Basics & Cabling
Research Task: Cat 5e vs. Cat 6 (Frequency & Speed)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Cable Type| Maximum Speed | Frequency (MHz)| Key Characteristics
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Cat 5e  | Up to 1 Gbps (Supports 2.5GBASE-T up to 100m)| 100 MHz|Thinner conductors (24 AWG), no internal spline.
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Cat 6 | Up to 10 Gbps (Supports 5GBASE-T up to 100m, 10G up to 55m)| 250 MHz | Thicker conductors (23 AWG), usually features an internal spline to reduce crosstalk.
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Support Scenario:
The Problem & Solution (In English):
"The copper cables (like Cat5e or Cat6) are strictly limited to a maximum distance of 100 meters (328 feet). 
Since the computer is located 120 meters away from the network closet, the signal suffers from severe 
attenuation (signal loss). This is what causes the connection to drop every 5 minutes. To fix this, 
we need to install a network switch/repeater in the middle, or use a Fiber Optic cable instead."
